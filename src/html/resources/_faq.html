FAQ
  • What is life insurance?

    Life insurance is a contract between the policyholder and the insurer, where the insurer agrees to pay a certain amount of money or a death benefit in the event of the policyholder's death

  • Why do I need life insurance?

    Life insurance provides financial protection for your loved ones in the event of your death, helping them cover expenses for essential needs such as mortgages, education, and everyday expenses

  • Which type of life insurance is right for me?

    Your best choice depends on your financial goals, needs, and circumstances. Various types of policies, such as term, permanent, and universal life insurance, have their own features and benefits

  • What are the main factors affecting the cost of life insurance?

    Key factors affecting the cost of life insurance include age, health status, coverage amount, policy term, and type of policy

  • Can I change my insurance policy in the future?

    Yes, most insurance companies offer a certain degree of flexibility, allowing you to modify your policy to meet your changing needs and circumstances

  • Can I receive a life insurance payout while still alive?

    Yes, some life insurance policies provide the option to receive a payout under certain circumstances, such as critical illness or disability

  • What happens to my insurance policy if I move abroad?

    Depending on the terms of your policy and the country of your residence, your insurance policy may be adapted or extended to accommodate new circumstances
